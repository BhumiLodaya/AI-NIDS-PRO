DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)

An attack that floods your server with fake traffic from multiple sources, overwhelming it so legitimate users can't access your services. Think of it like thousands of people blocking a store entrance.

Brute Force Attack

Attackers try thousands of password combinations to break into your system. Like a thief trying every key on a keyring until one works. Modern systems can try millions of passwords per second.

Botnet (Bot Attack)

A network of infected computers controlled by a hacker. These "zombie" devices can be used to send spam, steal data, or launch DDoS attacks without the owners knowing.

Infiltration

A stealthy attack where hackers gain unauthorized access to your network and remain hidden for extended periods, stealing data or planting malware. The digital equivalent of a spy inside your organization.

Port Scanning

Attackers probe your network to find open "doors" (ports) that might be vulnerable. Ports are like numbered windows on a building - scanning checks which ones are unlocked.

Web Attack

Exploits vulnerabilities in web applications (SQL injection, XSS, etc.). Like finding a backdoor in a website's code to steal data or take control of the server.

Benign Traffic

Normal, legitimate network activity from real users. This is the safe traffic you want flowing through your network - like customers entering your store during business hours.
